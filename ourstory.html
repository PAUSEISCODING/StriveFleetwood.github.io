Our Story

A little more about Strive.

More than coffee

Our journey began when a previously unused building was transformed into a vibrant community coffee shop and hub, thanks to funding support from Fylde Coast Medical Services (FCMS). FCMS is a social enterprise healthcare provider dedicated to improving health and wellbeing across local communities, and their support helped bring our vision to life.

We were also incredibly fortunate to receive support from a former pupil of Flakefleet Primary School who is now a National Director at Caffè Nero. From helping us design and furnish the coffee shop to providing staff training and ongoing business guidance, their generosity and expertise helped shape Strive into the welcoming space it is today.



But Strive is about far more than coffee.



Alongside our coffee shop , we provide a wide range of community services and activities designed to improve health, wellbeing, confidence, and connection. These include:

• Domestic abuse support groups
• Adult learning opportunities
• Community policing sessions
• Parenting and family support
• Health and wellbeing services
• Social groups and community meet-ups
• Children’s holiday clubs
• Social prescribing support
• Community fun days and events

Every day, we welcome a diverse mix of people through our doors - from school children receiving free breakfasts before class, to professionals hot-desking with a coffee, local workers taking a break, families meeting friends, and residents simply looking for company and conversation.

Many people describe Strive as feeling like the old-style community youth clubs of years gone by — a place where everyone is welcome, relationships matter, and community comes first. We like to think of ourselves as a modern-day “Family Club”.
